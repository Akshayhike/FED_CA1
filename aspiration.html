Challenges I might face

Embarking on a zero-waste lifestyle can definitely throw some curveballs my way. I've found that breaking old habits of convenience, like automatically reaching for a disposable coffee cup, is a real challenge, and it's not always easy to find those package-free personal care items or bulk food options here in Singapore, which sometimes means I have to plan my shopping trips more carefully. Plus, asking for no straw or bringing my own container for dabao can feel a little awkward at first! Figuring out how to repair something instead of just buying new often means learning a new skill. However, overcoming these hurdles is totally doable: I keep my reusables visible to build new habits, actively explore local bulk stores and online options for products, find that confidence and a polite smile help in social situations, and rely on online tutorials or local repair shops to fix items. Every small win, every item saved from the bin, feels like a huge victory, keeping me motivated to continue on this journey

How I will overcome these challenges

Overcoming these hurdles is totally doable. For those tricky habits, it's all about preparation: I keep my reusable bags, water bottle, and coffee cup right by the door or in my everyday bag so I never forget them. When it comes to finding those elusive package-free items, I've started exploring local spots like UnPackt or The Green Collective, and sometimes even ordering in bulk online. For the social aspect, I've learned that a polite smile and confidence go a long way, most hawkers and restaurant staff are actually quite understanding. And for repairs? YouTube tutorials are my best friend for simple fixes, and I'm always on the lookout for local repair cafes. Every small win, every item saved from the bin, feels like a huge victory, and that keeps me going!
